# Component Acceptance Gates

Detailed gates moved out of `SKILL.md` to keep the component skill lightweight. Load this only when producing or judging implementation-ready component specs.

## Hard Constraints

- Do not style a component until its business purpose, data grain, key fields, filter scope, and interaction state are known.
- Do not accept screenshot/sample-derived reusable component styles unless they have `styleGeneralization` or equivalent coverage: controlled pattern field, covered/composed/extension status, adaptive variables, renderer owner, fallback, and text-only reproduction proof. Raw screenshots, image paths, image embeddings, or OCR-only text are not durable component standards.
- Do not accept an implementation-ready component spec that lacks a `Positioning And Alignment Rules` / `位置排布与对齐规则` chapter with container variables, slot x/y positions, alignment, size tiers, and state geometry.
- Do not accept a component that has component-internal filters but lacks a `组件内筛选区 / 局部筛选区` section. It must define suitable filters, unsuitable filters, capsule/dropdown style, placement, size algorithm, title/unit/legend/metric relationship, and responsive collapse.
- Component-internal local filters affect only the current component or declared local group. They must not change page/global scope, permission scope, backend aggregation, pagination, export scope, or other components.
- Component-internal local filters use capsule sliding buttons for `2-4` short options, collapse to compact dropdown when options exceed `4` or width fails, and use a panel/popover/drawer trigger only for multiple local filter groups.
- Component-internal local filters must not cover or compress KPI values, chart plots, legends, axes, table headers, pagination, or state messages. Collapse the filter, secondary metrics, or actions before shrinking the core value/plot/table below its budget.
- Analysis & Insight components are a distinct report component family, even when implemented with `visualType: 'text-summary'`. A valid analysis/explanation component declares `analysisInsightContract` or equivalent metadata with subtype, conclusion, evidence, affected object, comparison/change value when relevant, action or drill path, confidence/definition/source/freshness when relevant, local-filter scope, tooltip payload, size tier, and states. Do not accept generic insight copy, essay-like explanations, full-card red warnings, decorative icons, or a card that says "发生变化/建议关注" without business evidence.
- Conclusion/insight/summary/interpretation cards are a controlled Analysis & Insight subtype, not generic AI insight copy. A valid conclusion card declares `analysisPerspective: conclusionInsight`, `analysisInsightContract.subtype: conclusion-card`, `conclusionCardPattern`, `conclusionEvidenceBodyMode`, `conclusionEvidenceBinding`, one lead conclusion, one primary evidence body, source/freshness, numeric display contracts for visible values, tooltip/detail route, and states. Do not accept cards whose only variation is icon, accent color, tab label, or decorative chart choice; KPI, trend, composition, formula, segment, or action evidence must be data-bound.
- Definition/help cards are a controlled Analysis & Insight subtype, not generic help copy. A valid definition/help card declares `analysisPerspective: definitionHelp`, `definitionHelpCardPattern`, `definitionHelpEvidenceBinding`, metric id/name, definition/formula/scope/source/freshness, numeric format contracts, denominator or explicit non-denominator reason when relevant, local-control scope when visible, tooltip/detail/dictionary route, size tier, and states. Do not accept polished说明卡, 口径卡, formula cards, trend/share/filter/example bodies, or icon-heavy help cards when they are decorative or screenshot-only.
- Action recommendation cards are a controlled decision-support card family, not generic AI recommendation copy. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: actionRecommendation`, `visualType: action-recommendation-card`, `actionRecommendationCardPattern`, `actionEvidenceBodyMode`, `actionRecommendationEvidenceBinding`, one trigger metric, one primary evidence body, `1-3` action items, source/freshness, numeric display contracts, tooltip/detail route, action route, size tier, and states. Do not accept a polished "下一步建议" card when the metric trigger, evidence chart/list, expected effect, owner/deadline/status, or CTA event is unbound; use `RPT-ACTION-EVIDENCE-MISSING`, `RPT-ACTION-NO-ROUTE`, `RPT-ACTION-NO-OWNER`, `RPT-ACTION-IMPACT-UNBOUND`, or `VIS-ACTION-AI-POLISH`.
- Review impact cards are controlled event-evidence components, not generic trend/KPI/conclusion skins. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: reviewImpact`, `reviewImpactCardPattern`, `reviewImpactEvidenceMode`, `reviewImpactEvidenceBinding`, event id/name/date or period, baseline method, metric formulas/units/source/freshness, exact-value/detail route, local-control scope, size tier, states, and fallback. Do not accept polished "活动复盘/效果提升" cards when event identity, before/after or control fields, event marker, baseline method, causal wording proof, or exact values are unbound; use `RPT-REVIEW-EVENT-MISSING`, `RPT-REVIEW-BASELINE-MISSING`, `RPT-REVIEW-CAUSALITY-UNPROVEN`, `RPT-REVIEW-METRIC-CONTRACT-MISSING`, `RPT-REVIEW-EXACT-VALUE-MISSING`, `VIS-REVIEW-EVIDENCE-SQUEEZED`, or `VIS-REVIEW-AI-POLISH`.
- Data quality trust cards are controlled audit-evidence components, not generic KPI/table/chart skins. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: dataQualityTrust`, `visualType: data-quality-card` or a justified real evidence renderer, `dataQualityTrustCardPattern`, `dataQualityEvidenceBinding`, data-quality task, data object scope, denominator policy, formula/rule references, source/freshness/batch metadata, numeric display contracts, tooltip/detail/source-lineage route, size tier, states, and fallback. Do not accept polished "数据质量/完整性/准确性/异常数据" cards when scope, denominator, rule proof, source freshness, exact exception rows, or action/closure route is unbound; use `RPT-DQ-*`, `VIS-DQ-AI-GENERIC`, `VIS-DQ-CARD-PILEUP`, `VIS-DQ-TRUST-OVERPAINT`, or `VIS-DQ-TABLE-DUMP`.
- Composite Panels / multi-component analysis cards are valid only when multiple child components answer one business question as one mini analysis loop: summary -> trend/structure -> contribution/exception -> detail/action. A valid Composite Panel declares `compositePanelContract`, one primary visual, default `2-3` child components, maximum `4` visible analytical child components unless a documented large-container exception exists, shared local filter scope, shared legend/unit behavior, child priority, linked hover/click context, child minimum sizes, responsive fallback, and partial child-state rules. Do not use a Composite Panel as a miniature dashboard, equal-weight chart collage, nested-card stack, or workaround for page-grid placement.
- Micro Dashboard Cards are a separate large-card pattern, not a Composite Panel exception. A valid Micro Dashboard Card declares `microDashboardCardPattern`, `microDashboardContract`, one shared management topic, KPI strip, `4-8` visible analytical child sections, parent minimum `680x620`, child roles/priorities/minimum sizes, exact-value/status path, shared filters/interactions, responsive fallback, and parent/child state rules. If the parent or any child fails its minimum, collapse lower-priority children, split, tab, drawer, or fullscreen before shrinking chart/table/text below readability.
- State feedback components must declare `stateFeedbackPattern`, `stateFeedbackContract.stateKind`, affected scope, title/reason/action skeleton, minimum size, geometry preservation, accessibility, and validation cases. No-permission states must not leak restricted totals, row counts, object names, chart silhouettes, trend remnants, or table headers that reveal unauthorized detail.
- Contracts must be executable, not decorative metadata. `analysisInsightContract`, `compositePanelContract`, KPI placement metadata, local-filter metadata, chart/table contracts, and similar declarations must map to actual DOM roles/classes, CSS/computed styles, renderer options, browser assertions, and screenshot/crop evidence when code or URL exists. If the contract exists only in config/prose, record `LAZY-CONTRACT-THEATER` and do not accept readiness.
- Component controls must pass ownership checks. A component may not duplicate template-owned refresh, download/export, copy/share, date/period, page/global filter, toolbar, or navigation controls unless `controlOwnership: "component"` or an equivalent explicit decision exists and the template-owned control is disabled/hidden. Otherwise record `VIS-DUPLICATE-CONTROL` or `RPT-SHELL-DUPLICATE`.
- Do not treat CSS flex/grid auto layout as sufficient by itself for dense report components. It must be backed by a measurable slot budget and a fallback when labels, values, icons, legends, controls, or state messages do not fit.
- Every implementation-ready component must declare a display budget for its family and a specific overflow strategy. Missing limits for text-summary items, cards/list rows, journey steps, KPI peers, chart categories/series/labels, table columns/rows, local-filter options, nodes/links, hierarchy depth, or annotations are acceptance gaps.
- Do not use generic "AI/SaaS polish" as a component style: purple-blue gradients, glass panels, black neon, glow buttons, floating decorative cards, oversized radius, abstract AI icons/3D assets, or particle/grid decoration require a named brand/template/sample rationale.
- When modern SaaS / BI Dashboard / UI Kit style is requested, components must map to the positive style contract: light/white analytical surfaces, thin borders, small-radius cards, stable UI Kit controls, shared token family, no nested-card look, no unrelated component pileup, and lightweight chart treatment. Missing mapping is `VIS-MODERN-BI-BASELINE-MISSING`; crowded cards or chart-heavy cards are `VIS-COMPONENT-PILEUP` or `VIS-CHART-OVERWEIGHT`.
- Component copy must be concrete. Titles, subtitles, CTAs, empty states, errors, and helper text must name the user action, data object, condition, owner, source, period, or next step. Generic words such as "赋能", "智能化", "一站式", "重新定义", "无缝", or "提升效率" are not enough.
- KPI cards, metric groups, chart blocks, and summary components must not be styled as generic metric shells. Every primary metric-bearing component must preserve visible or discoverable formula/denominator, grain, period, source/freshness, unit/precision, baseline, and owner/action context when the surface is implementation-ready.
- KPI overview cards are metric-card variants, not Micro Dashboard Cards or equal-weight chart collages. A valid card declares `kpiOverviewCardPattern`, one domain/topic, `2-5` ordered visible metrics, a lead metric when the pattern requires it, one local control group maximum before collapse, target/attainment fields for visible progress tracks, exact-value payload/route, and one compact evidence visual maximum. It fails when metrics are unbound decorative numbers, more than `5` metrics are squeezed into one card, multiple mini charts compete, or local controls change page/global scope.
- Single-indicator KPI grid cards are metric-card variants, not chart collages. A valid card declares `kpiSingleIndicatorLayoutMode`, one primary metric, one comparison/status, exactly one mini evidence visual, local control scope when visible, target/attainment fields when a footer exists, exact-value payload, and a fallback. It fails when one card contains multiple mini visuals, a local control has no data effect, the footer progress is not backed by target/attainment fields, or peer cards do not share height, padding, value baseline, and footer baseline.
- KPI goal execution cards are metric-card variants for target attainment, gap, progress, and milestone decisions, not generic gauge/progress/timeline decoration. A valid card declares `kpiGoalExecutionCardPattern`, `goalExecutionKind`, `goalDirection`, actual/target fields, required attainment/gap/progress/milestone fields, formula/denominator behavior, exactly one execution hero visual, comparison strip fields, footer evidence/source fields, exact-value payload, and deadline/remaining-time fields when visible.
- KPI time-series analysis cards are metric-card variants for trend, change, YoY/MoM, cycle, volatility, and forecast decisions, not generic sparkline decoration. A valid card declares `kpiTimeSeriesCardPattern`, `temporalAnalysisKind`, ordered time series, x/y fields, grain, latest period, direction semantics, required baseline/cycle/volatility/forecast fields for the selected pattern, tooltip payload, footer evidence/source fields, and exact-value payload.
- KPI comparison analysis cards are metric-card variants for direct comparison, group comparison, competitor comparison, benchmark position, and variance/gap decisions, not generic bar/radar/table variety. A valid card declares `kpiComparisonAnalysisCardPattern`, `comparisonAnalysisKind`, comparable subject grain, subject role fields, shared metric definition/unit/grain/filter scope, selected comparison/competitor/benchmark/variance fields, direction semantics, sort rule, tooltip payload, footer evidence/source fields, and exact-value payload.
- Ranking, leaderboard, and Top N cards are row/slot-aligned ranking components, not generic card lists or decorative bars. A valid card declares `rankingCardPattern`, `rankingEvidenceBinding`, ranking mode, source-side Top N or bounded-local exception, object id/name fields, value field, rank field, deterministic tie-break fields, visible-count budget, share denominator when share is visible, overflow strategy, tooltip payload, and detail/action route.
- Pareto cards are contribution-threshold ranking components, not ordinary bar charts. A valid card declares `paretoCardPattern`, `paretoEvidenceBinding`, additive value field, total field, contribution share, cumulative value/share, threshold share, boundary/key-factor field, long-tail policy, tooltip payload, and chart/table/detail fallback.
- Decomposition, attribution, contribution, and hierarchy cards are explanation components, not decorative diagram cards. A valid card declares `decompositionAttributionCardPattern`, `decompositionAttributionEvidenceBinding`, explanation mode, root metric/unit/value, selected-pattern fields, `signRule`, `reconciliationPolicy`, residual/`其他` handling, tooltip/detail route, size family, and fallback. Attribution wording requires an explicit attribution method or diagnostic model; otherwise downgrade to contribution, driver, or decomposition wording.
- Distribution, interval, density, and boxplot cards are statistical evidence components, not generic chart variety. A valid card declares `distributionAnalysisCardPattern`, `distributionAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, sample grain, sample count, metric/unit, selected bucket/density/quantile/boxplot/heatmap/geography fields, interval policy, missing/zero/out-of-range policy, tooltip/detail route, size family, and fallback. Density and boxplot wording is invalid without sample-count and estimator/statistics proof.
- Time-pattern cards are temporal-evidence components, not ordinary trend tiles or decorative calendars. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: timePattern`, `timePatternCardPattern`, `timePatternEvidenceBinding`, ordered grain/date/slot fields, period start/end, metric/unit/aggregation, numeric display contract, missing-vs-zero policy, local-control scope, exact-value/detail route, density/fallback rules, and pattern-specific calendar, schedule-lane, heatmap, cumulative, peak-valley, or forecast fields.
- Multidimensional feature, profile, radar, and tag cards are feature-evidence components, not decorative persona cards, ornamental radar charts, or random tag clouds. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: multiDimensionalProfile`, controlled `multiDimensionalFeatureCardPattern`, `multiDimensionalFeatureEvidenceBinding`, object/dimension/tag fields, scale policy for radar or parallel profiles, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer owner, size family, state coverage, and fallback. It fails when object identity is fake decoration, radar dimensions lack comparable scale, tags have no source/status/rule fields, exact values are hidden, or profile/label evidence is screenshot-only.
- Anomaly, risk, warning, and outlier analysis cards are diagnostic-lens components, not generic alert skins. A valid card declares `anomalyAnalysisCardPattern`, `anomalyAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, one diagnostic lens, severity/status dictionary, current/baseline or threshold evidence, occurrence/update/freshness time, affected-object or impact fields when visible, tooltip/detail/action route, size family, and fallback. A polished abnormality card with no baseline, threshold, time, or next-step path is `RPT-ANOMALY-EVIDENCE-MISSING` or `RPT-ANOMALY-NO-ACTION`.
- Matrix decision, quadrant, priority, and strategy-layer cards are decision-classification components, not generic scatter/matrix/card variety. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: matrixDecision`, controlled `matrixDecisionCardPattern`, `matrixDecisionEvidenceBinding`, one decision task, object grain, axes/dimensions/priority/layer fields required by the selected pattern, deterministic classification rule, threshold or band rule when visible, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer owner, size family, state coverage, and fallback. A polished matrix without data-driven classification is `RPT-MATRIX-CLASSIFICATION-MISSING`; hidden exact values are `RPT-MATRIX-EXACT-VALUE-MISSING`; decorative generic matrix polish is `VIS-MATRIX-AI-POLISH`.
- Market movement, quote, K-line, breadth, watchlist, and volatility cards are market-evidence components, not generic trend/KPI/chart skins. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: marketMovement`, `visualType: market-analysis-card`, controlled `marketAnalysisCardPattern`, `marketAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, instrument identity when instrument-level, quote/OHLC/breadth/volatility/risk-return fields required by the selected pattern, market rise/fall color convention, source/freshness, tooltip/detail route, local-control scope, renderer owner, size family, state coverage, and fallback. A polished card with fake sparklines, invalid candles, missing quote/source/time, hidden delayed/closed/halted state, or undeclared color convention is `RPT-MARKET-*` or `VIS-MARKET-*`.
- Detail evidence cards are row-proof components, not generic white-card polish. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: detailEvidence`, controlled `detailEvidenceCardPattern`, `detailEvidenceBinding`, row grain, primary key, identity fields, default sort or event order, status/severity dictionary when visible, source/freshness when relevant, tooltip payload, exact-value/detail/export route, local-control scope, renderer owner, density limit, state coverage, and fallback. A polished 明细卡, 列表卡, 样本卡, 日志卡, or 下钻卡 with no row proof, no exact route, unbounded rows/columns/steps, duplicate shell controls, or decorative chart/list variety is `RPT-DETAIL-*` or `VIS-DETAIL-*`.
- Relationship, correlation, association, and influence analysis cards are relation-evidence components, not generic network/chart variety. A valid card declares `relationshipAnalysisCardPattern`, `relationshipAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, `analysisPerspective: relationshipInfluence`, relation task, entity grain, source/target or pair/node/edge fields, metric/strength/direction/method fields, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer owner, size family, and fallback. Influence or causality wording without method/model/business-rule proof is `RPT-RELATION-CAUSALITY-UNPROVEN`; missing pattern/data/method evidence is `RPT-RELATION-PATTERN-MISSING` or `RPT-RELATION-DATA-MISSING`.
- Process, step, node, and bottleneck analysis cards are process-evidence components, not decorative workflow skins. A valid card declares `processAnalysisCardPattern`, `processAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, `analysisPerspective: processBottleneck`, one process task, stage/node/status/timing/bottleneck fields required by the selected pattern, order/threshold/denominator rules, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer owner, size family, and fallback. Bottleneck wording without baseline/threshold/comparison plus impact or action evidence is `RPT-BOTTLENECK-EVIDENCE-MISSING` or `RPT-BOTTLENECK-NO-ACTION`; missing process pattern/data/order evidence is `RPT-PROCESS-METHOD-MISSING`, `RPT-PROCESS-DATA-MISSING`, or `RPT-PROCESS-ORDER-MISSING`.
- Conversion, retention, loss, and stage conversion analysis cards are conversion-evidence components, not decorative funnel skins. A valid card declares `conversionRetentionCardPattern`, `conversionRetentionEvidenceBinding`, `analysisPerspective: conversionRetention`, one conversion-retention task, required stage/cohort/window/loss/path/comparison fields, numerator/denominator/formula rules, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer owner, size family, and fallback. Funnel or retention wording without shared cohort, stage order, retention window, denominator, or exact-value route is `RPT-CONVERSION-COHORT-MISSING`, `RPT-CONVERSION-FORMULA-MISSING`, or `RPT-RETENTION-WINDOW-MISSING`.
- Axis-line diagnostic KPI cards are a KPI-card subtype, not decorative chart tiles. A valid card declares `kpiCardPattern: horizontal-axis-line-trend`, `kpiAxisLineEvidenceMode`, ordered x/y fields, y-axis unit, tooltip payload, ECharts renderer ownership, and any target/reference/threshold/phase/comparison-series fields used by the visible chart. It fails when axes, grid, target labels, threshold bands, or phase marks are not data-driven.
- Axis-bar diagnostic KPI cards are a KPI-card subtype, not decorative chart tiles or ordinary mini bar strips. A valid card declares `kpiCardPattern: horizontal-axis-bar-compare`, `kpiAxisBarEvidenceMode`, ordered category/value fields, x-axis unit, sort rule, visible bar limit, tooltip payload, ECharts renderer ownership, and any target/reference/threshold/change-rate/comparison-series fields used by the visible chart. It fails when category labels, value labels, target/reference/threshold marks, dual-series bars, or change-rate sidebars are not data-driven.
- Axis-scatter diagnostic KPI cards are a KPI-card subtype, not decorative dot fields or ordinary scatter blocks. A valid card declares `kpiCardPattern: horizontal-axis-scatter-diagnostic`, `kpiScatterEvidenceMode`, object grain, x/y fields and units, point count, density/label strategy, tooltip payload, ECharts renderer ownership, and any trendline/reference/threshold/quadrant/category/size fields used by the visible chart. It fails when the card has fewer than two numeric metrics, anonymous points without object grain, unmanaged dense points, or non-data-driven trendlines/quadrants/thresholds.
- Spatial-map diagnostic KPI cards are a KPI-card subtype, not decorative basemaps. A valid card declares `kpiCardPattern: horizontal-spatial-map-diagnostic`, `kpiMapEvidenceMode`, geography decision task, region code or lon/lat fields, map resource/projection, visualMap/legend strategy, missing-geo handling, tooltip payload, renderer ownership, and any target-gap/change/category/point fields used by the visible map. It fails when geography is only a label, the map is stretched, the resource/projection is unknown, or the map lacks a real spatial decision.
- Spatial/map/geographic analysis cards are a card-level spatial evidence package, not generic map charts or decorative basemaps. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: spatialDistribution`, controlled `spatialAnalysisCardPattern`, `spatialAnalysisEvidenceBinding`, geography grain, region code or lon/lat/origin-destination fields, map resource/projection, visualMap/legend strategy, missing-geo policy, density and label rules, tooltip/detail route, renderer ownership, state coverage, and fallback. It fails when geography is only an org label, exact values are hidden, the map is screenshot-only, or side metrics/controls do not answer one spatial question.
- Population/object/customer segmentation/object detail cards are people/object evidence components, not decorative persona cards or miniature dashboard collages. A valid card declares `analysisPerspective: populationObject`, controlled `populationObjectCardPattern`, `populationObjectEvidenceBinding`, object grain, identity or aggregate policy, denominator policy when shares are visible, task-specific segment/RFM/lifecycle/behavior/channel/geo/relation/timeline/score/risk fields, density and label rules, tooltip/detail/action route, renderer owner, size family, state coverage, and fallback. It fails when identity is fake decoration, shares lack denominator, evidence is screenshot-only, object 360 content becomes a pileup, or exact values/action paths are hidden.
- Paired comparison diagnostic KPI cards are a KPI-card subtype, not two unrelated metric tiles. A valid card declares `kpiCardPattern: paired-comparison-diagnostic`, `kpiComparisonEvidenceMode`, left/right pane fields, shared metric definition/unit/grain/filter scope, baseline labels, delta/gap/conclusion field, tooltip payload, and optional mini evidence fields. It fails when the two panes are not comparable, the central `VS` rail is decorative, or the bottom conclusion cannot be computed from declared fields.
- Do not add chart variety for decoration. A chart/table choice must serve a specific task such as target gap, trend, ranking, composition, distribution, relationship, driver diagnosis, abnormality, detail, or action. Otherwise record `RPT-DECORATIVE-CHART`.
- Visual treatment must not compete with data reading. Gradients, glass, glow, illustration, large shadows, and high-saturation decorative colors fail when they reduce numeric hierarchy, axis/legend readability, anomaly visibility, table scanning, or exact-value access.
- A light-surface UI Kit component can still fail when hierarchy is flat, all cards have equal emphasis, or chart/list/table variants are added for polish instead of decision roles. Use `VIS-HIERARCHY-FLAT` and `VIS-GENERIC-SAAS-SHELL` when this happens.
- Report components that show a result must expose the next step or evidence path: tooltip, detail table, drawer, drilldown, export, owner/action, annotation, or runbook. A polished result card with no action path is `RPT-NO-ACTION`.
- Prototype or mock component states must not imply unrealistically clean performance. All-green/all-up KPIs, perfectly smooth trends, balanced shares, and no missing/zero/extreme states should be marked `RPT-TOO-CLEAN-DATA` unless the source evidence proves that reality.
- Icon-only and decorative components are not accepted when they carry decision-critical meaning without labels, tooltips, data, or accessible names.
- Motion must communicate state, feedback, hierarchy, loading, selection, or transition. Decorative movement that distracts from scanning or has no reduced-motion behavior fails component style QA.
- Do not accept a polished component that is unreasonable for the task. Use `DESIGN-*` findings when a chart should be a table, a dense component needs drilldown/fullscreen, a component duplicates another message, or the style hides the user's decision-critical value.
- Do not hide decision-critical labels, units, warnings, or values without a hover/focus/click disclosure path.
- Do not let ECharts, S2, SVG, canvas, or custom diagrams mount into a zero-size or unstable parent block or sub-block container.
- When a component is a standard ECharts chart, implement it as an ECharts chart: project wrapper or `echarts.init`, data-driven `option`/`series`, normal `setOption`/update/resize, and ECharts tooltip/legend/emphasis behavior. Do not import ECharts while hand-drawing bars, lines, pies, gauges, maps, parallel-coordinate axes/lines, sunburst arcs, relation graph nodes/edges, axes, or legends with SVG/HTML/CSS/canvas.
- Standard ECharts charts must pass runtime lifecycle acceptance when code or URL exists: non-zero measurable mount body before init, option updates through `setOption` or wrapper equivalent, container/visibility resize through `ResizeObserver` or documented equivalent hooks, post-hidden-state resize for tabs/drawers/fullscreen, and observer/listener/instance cleanup on unmount. A fixed design-width page can pass `container-resize-safe` but cannot claim `viewport-responsive` without breakpoint/reflow proof.
- When a component is restored from HTML/source, do not treat sample `<svg>`, `<canvas>`, CSS bars, DOM arcs, or absolute-positioned chart marks as reusable chart code. They are visual/config evidence only. Standard chart components must be rebuilt through ECharts data-driven options/series unless a named custom-diagram exception is declared before implementation.
- Hand-authored SVG/canvas is allowed only for icons, logos, trend arrows, decorative assets, or explicitly named custom diagrams/graphics that are not standard charts. ECharts output using `renderer: 'svg'` is valid because ECharts owns the generated SVG.
- Do not stretch SVG, canvas, ECharts custom graphics, gauges, maps, radar, sunburst, flow paths, or generated decorative geometry with independent X/Y scaling. Preserve geometry with a canonical `viewBox`, `preserveAspectRatio="xMidYMid meet"`, CSS `aspect-ratio`, or a measured inner fit box using `scale = min(containerWidth / designWidth, containerHeight / designHeight)`.
- Do not use CSS `transform: scaleX/scaleY`, `object-fit: fill`, or raw `width: 100%; height: 100%` SVG/canvas stretching when the graphic shape has business meaning. Center/letterbox inside the assigned viewport or redesign the graphic for the actual aspect ratio.
- Radar charts must declare dimension count, visible series count, shared score scale, standardization rule for mixed raw units, radius/center, ring count, dimension-label gap, and value-label strategy before styling. Do not accept radar with `> 10` dimensions, `> 3` visible series, mixed raw units plotted directly, or an elliptical/stretched coordinate system.
- Scatter/bubble charts must declare X metric, Y metric, units, axis range/baseline behavior, point count, point density strategy, point or bubble size mapping, label strategy, reference-line/quadrant behavior, legend separation, and tooltip payload before styling. Do not accept scatter with only one numeric metric, missing X/Y units, dense points without sampling/aggregation/zoom, all-point labels in non-sparse charts, or unmanaged DOM overlays for points/axes/reference lines.
- Parallel coordinates must declare the multi-metric object comparison task, object/sample id/name fields, `3-12` dimension fields, dimension order, per-axis unit/range/scale direction, standardization or independent-axis rule, sample count, line opacity tiers, highlight/group semantics, axis spacing budget, brush behavior when present, legend/filter separation, tooltip payload, and table/scatter/bar fallback before styling. Do not accept parallel coordinates for `1-2` metrics, single ranking, exact value audit, `>12` dimensions without filtering/scroll, excessive sample lines without opacity/sampling/aggregation, or unmanaged hand-drawn axes/lines when ECharts parallel is claimed.
- Map/geographic charts must declare the geography decision task, region code or longitude/latitude fields, map resource/projection, fitBounds/aspect-ratio behavior, map layer type, visualMap/legend strategy, point/flow density, bubble or flow-size mapping when present, label strategy, missing-geo handling, and tooltip payload before styling. Do not accept maps with no real geography field, purely decorative basemaps, stretched geography, all-region/all-point labels, dense points/flows without clustering/heatmap/TopN, or unmanaged hand-drawn map layers when ECharts map is claimed.
- Candlestick/K-line charts must declare ordered time, `open/high/low/close`, unit, market rise/fall color convention, price range padding, visible candle count, candle/body width, main/volume/indicator height budget, MA/indicator count, axis label sampling, crosshair/tooltip payload, dataZoom behavior, and missing OHLC/volume handling before styling. Do not accept candlestick charts with only one value per time point, unsorted OHLC rows, invalid OHLC relationships, dense candles without dataZoom/recent-window fallback, overloaded technical indicators, or unmanaged hand-drawn candles when ECharts candlestick is claimed.
- Boxplot charts must declare raw-sample or precomputed statistical contract, sample count, Q1/median/Q3/IQR, whisker and outlier rule, unit, axis range, category count, grouped-series count, box/whisker geometry, outlier density strategy, label strategy, and tooltip payload before styling. Do not accept boxplots for single aggregate ranking, sample-too-small categories without fallback, unspecified outlier rules, grouped series beyond `S > 3` without scroll/fallback, all-stat labels on every category, or unmanaged hand-drawn boxes when ECharts boxplot is claimed.
- Matrix/time/calendar/correlation heatmaps must declare row dimension, column dimension, value metric, aggregation grain, unit, color-scale type, visualMap range, missing-vs-zero encoding, row/column count, cell size, label sampling, value-label threshold, highlight rule, and tooltip payload before styling. Do not accept non-geographic heatmaps for one-dimensional ranking, exact small-number comparison, tiny value variance, unbounded row/column cardinality, unclear color semantics, all-cell permanent labels, missing values rendered as zero, or unmanaged hand-drawn heatmap cells when ECharts heatmap/calendar heatmap is claimed.

## Avoid

- Do not use decorative cards, gradients, or visual noise when the component's job is analytical scanning.
- Do not make components look "premium" by adding generic gradients, glass, glow, huge radii, abstract AI imagery, or ornamental animation.
- Do not make a component look modern by adding more mini cards, badges, charts, or local controls than its display budget supports.
- Do not accept generic empty copy in component headings, helper text, empty states, or errors.
- Do not use charts as visual variety when a table, detail drawer, decomposition, or action block is the actual decision support.
- Do not hide metric口径, source/freshness, baseline, anomaly marker, or owner/action context merely to keep a KPI/chart card cleaner.
- Do not add internal chart/table/card titles that duplicate the page or block title.
- Do not make a component look compact by making it too narrow, too small, crowded, or unreadable.
